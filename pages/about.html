Research Summary

My research centers on developing efficient and interpretable machine learning algorithms, with a particular focus on dimensionality reduction and the analysis of high-dimensional data.

I work on approaches such as manifold learning, sparse representations, and spectral methods to reveal underlying structure in complex datasets.

By integrating concepts from optimization and statistical learning theory, my work aims to improve both the scalability and theoretical grounding of these algorithms.

The ultimate goal is to design dimensionality reduction techniques that are robust, generalizable, and practically applicable across diverse domains, including bioinformatics and pattern recognition.
